Daily Practices & Protocols to Improve Focus

A summary of Huberman Lab Podcast #88 | Focus Toolkit: Tools to Improve Your Focus & Concentration

#1 Most Important Protocol: Get high quality sleep of sufficient duration (6-8 hours for most people). The effectiveness of all the other tools and protocols mentioned below depends on this.

Protocols to Rapidly Increase Focus:

1) Cold Exposure: E.g. Cold Shower (1-5 min), Ice Bath

  • Substantially increases dopamine, cortisol, and epinephrine levels
  • Should be uncomfortably cold, but not so cold that it becomes unsafe to stay in for more than 5 minutes
  • 00:55:46 | Cold Exposure

2) Visual Focus: Overt or Covert Focused Visual Attention

  • Visually focus on one location for 30 seconds to 3 minutes
  • Target of attention could be anything in nearby vicinity
  • Remain relaxed and try to limit the frequency of blinking
  • IMPORTANT: Refocus on your chosen visual target when your attention drifts
  • Do 5-10 minutes before an intense bout of focused effort
  • 01:16:46 | Overt Visual Focus
  • 01:20:42 | Covert Visual Focus

3) 40 Hz Binaural Beats

  • Listen to binaural beats 5-10 minutes before an intense bout of focused effort
  • 40 Hz Binaural Beats
  • 00:18:11 | Binaural Beats

4) White, Pink, and Brown Noise

  • Listen to binaural beats 5-10 minutes before an intense bout of focused effort
  • Improves ability to transition into concentrated states
  • I've found Brown noise in particular to be a great tool for blocking out distracting noise during the actual focus bout
  • White Noise
  • Pink Noise
  • Brown Noise (my personal favorite)
  • 00:20:54 | White, Pink, and Brown Noise

Daily Practices to Improve Natural Ability to Focus:

1) Concentration Meditation:

  • 10-15 minutes in duration
  • Sit or lay down
  • Focus on your breathing and bring your attention to a point behind your forehead
  • Your attention will most likely drift off at some point during the session, but this is ok
  • The main objective here is to refocus your attention as needed back to the point behind your forehead
  • Don't do this practice too close to bedtime since it can disrupt sleep in some individuals
  • 01:01:19 | Concentration Meditation

2) Non-Sleep Deep Rest (NSDR) or Yoga Nidra

  • Varies from around 20 minutes to 1 hour in duration
  • Do before bed to relax, or during the day to increase alertness if sleep deprived
  • Can replenish sleep debt to a limited extent
  • 30 Minute Yoga Nidra Session
  • 01:07:40 | Non-Sleep Deep Rest

3) Hypnosis (Hyper Focus)

  • Combines the focus and alertness achieved via concentration mediation with the relaxation achieved via NSDR
  • Defined as a state of deep immersive focus on a task
  • Hypnosis, also referred to as 'Hyper Focus', is the target mental state to reach while in Hyper Focus mode when using HyperChill.io
  • Hypnosis App Recommended By Andrew Huberman
  • 01:12:13 | Hypnosis (Hyper Focus)

Nutrition-Based Tools:

1) Morning Fasting

  • Do not eat (or limit your intake of) food for initial focus bouts
  • It's ok to ingest water and caffeine during this period
  • 00:36:53 | Fasted & Fed States

2) Avoid High Intake of Simple Sugars & Processed Foods

  • Avoid foods such as ice cream, candy, soda, etc.
  • 00:45:52 | Foods to Eliminate

3) Eat a Small to Moderate Sized Lunch & Incorporate Foods Rich in Tyrosine

  • You may experience a small dip in energy soon after eating, but your energy should rebound above baseline later on provided that you haven't overeaten
  • Eat foods with a significant amount of tyrosine, E.g. Parmesan cheese, beef, soybeans, nuts, etc.
  • 00:46:26 | Foods to Improve Focus

4) Don't overeat

  • Eating too much will rapidly diminish your ability to focus due to the triggering of the parasympathetic nervous system
  • In general, eating in moderation will provide your brain with the glucose necessary to perform optimally, while also retaining a certain degree of sympathetic activation (alertness)
  • 00:40:00 | Why Overeating Leads to Fatigue

Supplement-Based Tools:

1) Omega-3 essential fatty acids

  • EPA fatty acids (1-3g/ day): Improves mood and cognitive function
  • Sources: Fish oil, cod liver oil, salmon, etc.
  • 01:24:43 | Omega-3 Essential Fatty Acids

2) Creatine Monohydrate

  • Acts as a fuel for neuron function
  • Improves cognitive performance
  • Most individuals ingest ~5g/ day
  • 01:27:28 | Creatine Monohydrate

3) Caffeine

  • Boosts both mental and physical performance
  • Increases density and efficacy of dopamine receptors in the brain
  • Dosage depends on your current tolerance to caffeine
  • General range of effectiveness: 100-400mg/ day
  • Delay intake to 90-120 minutes after waking, unless working out immediately after waking
  • Try to avoid ingesting caffeine after 1-2 pm to ensure sleep quality is unaffected
  • Methods to ingest caffeine: Coffee, yerba mate, tea, capsule form, dark chocolate, etc.
  • WORD OF CAUTION: Avoid the smoked version of yerba mate due to the presence of carcinogenic (cancer promoting) compounds
  • 00:47:53 | Caffeine

4) Acetylcholine

  • Improves neuroplasticity (learning) by directly increasing focus
  • Greatly increases one's ability to concentrate
  • Ingest Alpha-GPC supplement to acutely increase acetylcholine (300-600mg/ day)
  • Take 10-20 minutes before a workout or focus bout, and early in the day if possible
  • Combine with caffeine for synergistic effect that maximizes focus
  • WORD OF CAUTION: A recent study has suggested that Alpha-GPC may increase the risk of stroke via increases in TMAO (cardiovascular system related marker)
  • Andrew Huberman believes data is inconclusive, but ingests 600mg of garlic along with Alpha-GPC to offset any potential increase in TMAO as an extra precautionary measure
  • 01:29:10 | Acetylcholine

5) L-Tyrosine

  • Boosts concentration and focus by increasing dopamine transmission
  • Ingest ~500mg/ day in capsule form
  • Take early in the day
  • 01:33:52 | L-Tyrosine

6) Phenylethylamine

  • Increases dopamine transmission similar to L-tyrosine, however can act as an alternative
  • Ingest ~500mg/ day in capsule form
  • Effects don't last as long as L-tyrosine, can take multiple times, E.g. Once at beginning of work session, and again in the middle
  • 01:42:56 | Phenylethylamine

End Notes:

  • There is always a gradual ramping up of focus and concentration at the beginning of any focus bout
  • Focus and concentration are abilities that can be improved through neuroplasticity (learning & practice)
  • Andrew Huberman ingests 500mg of L-tyrosine, 300mg of Alpha-GPC, and a cup of coffee early in the day about 4 days/ week
  • Andrew Huberman recommends not combining more than 2-3 tools/ protocols at any one time; however there are no strict requirements :)
  • Utilize supplements to teach yourself how to focus and concentrate more deeply so that they're not a requirement for every focus bout
  • For further information about nutrition and supplementation, check out examine.com

About

Hello! Welcome to HyperChill.io. My name is Grant and I designed this web app to help myself stay on task while in college. After noticing a phenomenal improvement in my own productivity as a result of this app, I knew it had to be shared with the world.

This web app was intended to be an alternative to the Pomodoro Method. It offers a more flexible and adaptable approach to studying which better takes into account the unpredictability of life. Rather than letting a timer dictate when you can take a break, the stopwatch embedded within this app highlights the amount of time you've spent studying and lets YOU decide when to take a break based on how you're feeling.

How It Works

1. Optional - Set a target time (in hours) that you want for a studying session

2. Press 'Start' to start the session

3. After you've been studying for a while and need a break, press 'Stop' to enter 'Chill Time'

4. Press 'Start' once again to re-enter 'Hyper Focus' mode

5. Repeat steps 3-4 as needed until you're done with the session entirely, at which point you can hit 'End' or 'End Session'

Benefits & Features

Defeat Procrastination:

  • 99% of the time, procrastination happens subconsciously. When you hit 'Start' or 'Stop', you're prompted to consciously acknowledge what you're doing so that you don't find yourself having spent 3 out of the past 4 hours on TikTok even though you intended to get all your homework done.

Track your Progress:

  • Use the Report feature to explore a graphical representation of your Hyper Focus/ Chill Time intervals for each day, week, and month. Use this information along with the Notes feature to detect patterns and make any necessary changes to maximize productivity.

Discover Tips:

  • Visit our Blog page to discover scientifically-informed protocols for maximizing productivity from leading voices such as Andrew Huberman, Sam Harris, and more!
